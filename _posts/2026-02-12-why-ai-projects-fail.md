*Based on RAND Corporation Research Report (RRA2680-1)*

## The Hard Truth About AI Project Failure Rates

Here's a sobering statistic that should make every tech leader pause: **more than 80% of AI projects fail** — that's **twice the failure rate** of traditional IT projects!

RAND Corporation researchers interviewed 65 data scientists and engineers with 5+ years of experience to uncover why so many AI initiatives crash and burn. The findings are eye-opening — and more importantly, **entirely preventable**.

## The 5 Root Causes of AI Project Failure

### 1. Miscommunication About the Problem

**The #1 killer of AI projects?** Teams don't actually understand — or can't clearly communicate — what problem they're trying to solve.

> "Industry stakeholders often misunderstand — or miscommunicate — what problem needs to be solved using AI."

Before writing a single line of code, make sure everyone is aligned on the **why**.

### 2. Insufficient Training Data

You can't train an effective AI model without quality data. Period.

Many organizations dive into AI projects without:
- Enough data volume
- Proper data quality
- Relevant data types

**No data = No AI magic**

### 3. Chasing Shiny Technology

Are you solving a real problem... or just playing with the latest tech?

> "AI projects fail because the organization focuses more on using the latest and greatest technology than on solving real problems for their intended users."

**Technology should serve the mission — not the other way around!**

### 4. Infrastructure Gaps

Even brilliant AI models fail when organizations lack:
- Proper data governance
- Deployment pipelines
- Scalable infrastructure

Building AI without infrastructure is like buying a Ferrari without a road to drive it on.

### 5. Problems Too Difficult for AI

Sometimes the technology is applied to problems that are simply too difficult for current AI capabilities to solve. Not every problem is a good fit for AI solutions.

## How to Make Your AI Projects Succeed

The good news? Each of these failure modes is **entirely preventable**. Stay tuned for our follow-up post on expert recommendations for AI project success.

## Key Takeaways

- **Align first, build second** — Get everyone on the same page about the problem
- **Data is everything** — Invest in data quality and quantity before model development
- **Problem over technology** — Choose tools that fit the problem, not the other way around
- **Build the foundation** — Infrastructure investments pay dividends
- **Know AI's limits** — Some problems aren't suited for AI solutions
